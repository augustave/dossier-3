RUBRIC · DESIGN SYSTEM Overview · i
Principium

The System

An aesthetic recovered from a single document and abstracted into a kit. Illuminated manuscript meets technical brief.

RUBRIC is a visual design language for documents that need to feel weighed and lasting — doctrine, briefs, manifestos, charters, reports, any artifact meant to be read once with full attention and kept. It descends from the illuminated manuscript and the typeset technical brief: parchment grounds, a chiselled Roman display face, monastic margins, untranslated marginalia, and a forged metal mark too dark to read unless you already know what you are looking at.

The name is taken from the practice of rubrication — the red headings and instructions that medieval scribes added to a black-ink page. A rubric is both a piece of red text and a rule. The system is exactly that: a set of rules, marked in a single rationed red.

Its character is restraint. Two grounds, three typefaces, square corners, one accent colour per page, and the discipline of stillness over motion. Everything ceremonial is set in capitals and given room to breathe; everything structural is a hairline. The whitespace is the silence the document speaks into.

This specification is itself built in RUBRIC — every page, token, and component on the following sheets is a live demonstration of the system it documents. To see the language, read the spec.

Use it for: long-form reading documents, design or brand doctrine, reports and white papers, editorial covers, certificate and charter layouts, and any interface that benefits from gravity and literacy over speed and friendliness. Do not use it for high-frequency dashboards, consumer onboarding, or anything that must feel light and fast.
RUBRIC · DESIGN SYSTEM Laws · ii
Regula

The Eight Laws

The rules that separate RUBRIC from a moodboard. Break one and the document stops being in-system.
  1. I
    Two grounds only
    A page is either vellum (warm parchment, ink text) or sable (near-black, vellum text). There is no third surface and no pure white. Light pages carry faint radial stains; dark pages carry faint gold and oxide. The ground is never flat.
  2. II
    Three faces, no more
    Cinzel for everything ceremonial (titles, labels, numerals), EB Garamond for body, Cormorant Garamond italic for thesis lines and classical-language text. A sans-serif is permitted for technical specs only — never narrative, never ceremonial.
  3. III
    Square corners, always
    Every border, pill, swatch, and box is border-radius: 0. Rounding reads as software friendliness; the system refuses it. Corners are cut, not softened.
  4. IV
    Tonal-on-tonal marks
    The mark is recovered from its ground, not stamped onto it — forged silver on near-black, nearly invisible until the eye adjusts. The reader who cannot find the mark is reading someone else's document.
  5. V
    One rationed accent per page
    Cross Red appears at most once per page, never as decoration. When it shows up, it means something. Bull Gold is reserved for covers and callouts. Metals are scarce by rule.
  6. VI
    Tracking widens as type shrinks
    The smaller and more ceremonial a line, the more air between its letters — from 0.04em body to 0.55em eyebrows. This single rule is the most recognisable signature of the system.
  7. VII
    A classical eyebrow over a plain title
    Each section opens with a short label in a classical register (a Latin word, a category, or a code), then the plain-English title, then a one-line italic thesis stating the single idea the section defends. Always centred.
  8. VIII
    Print-first, stillness over motion
    The canonical artifact prints clean to paper, one section per page. Motion is not a feature; its absence is. The only permitted movement is a single slow reveal on load. Heavy things do not bounce.

RUBRIC · DESIGN SYSTEM Type · iv
Litterae

Three Faces, No More

Cinzel for the chisel, Garamond for the page, Cormorant for the whisper.
The three faces

Cinzel — a Trajan-equivalent inscriptional capital — carries titles, eyebrows, labels, codes, and numerals; always uppercase, always tracked. EB Garamond — a Caslon-equivalent oldstyle — carries narrative body, justified with hyphenation. Cormorant Garamond italic carries thesis lines and classical-language text. A geometric sans-serif is permitted for dense technical specs only.

RUBRIC · DESIGN SYSTEM Layout · v
Mensura

Measure & Margin

A single narrow column, monastic margins, hairline rules. The page is the unit, not the viewport.
The page grid
Rule   Content caps at 880px wide and is centred on the dark void. Each page is a full 100vh sheet with 96px top/bottom and 88px side padding, and breaks to its own page in print. One column. No sidebars.
Single column
max-width 880 · pad 96 × 88
One idea per sheet
— content well —
Vertical rhythm
Rule   Sections fall in a fixed cadence: a header rule, then eyebrow → 14px → title → 14px → thesis → 48px → body. Sub-headings get 44px of air above. Spacing is generous and consistent; density is never the goal.
  • Hairline borders, low opacity Dividers are 1px at 18–25% opacity in warm earth or brass. They organise without shouting — never a hard black line on the light ground.
  • Centred ceremonial, justified body Eyebrows, titles, and theses centre on the column axis. Body copy justifies to both edges for the texture of a set page.
  • The page is the unit Layout is composed in sheets, not scroll regions. Each sheet carries one idea and prints as one page.
RUBRIC · DESIGN SYSTEM Mark · vi
Signum

The Forged Mark

A method, not a logo. Silver on near-black, beveled and aged, sized by ceremony.

The mark is built from sixteen flat facets — two per point of an eight-point star — laid on two alternating silver gradients so adjacent facets catch light from opposite directions. That alternation, plus mitered joins and a hairline stroke, reads as forged metal rather than flat fill. A fractal-noise patina ages the surface; a tiny dark core anchors the centre.

Construction rules
  • Silver, never gold The mark is pewter and steel; gold is reserved for ceremonial type. A gold mark would break the rationing of metal.
  • Two gradients, opposed Light enters from 20%/0%→80%/100% on one set and the mirror on the other. Single-gradient marks look stamped; opposed gradients look forged.
  • Sized by ceremony Large on a cover (168px), small at a close (84px). The mark grows for arrival and shrinks for farewell — never decorative repetition.
  • Geometry is swappable Star, cross, rosette, monogram — any centred radial form forges the same way. The method is the system; the shape belongs to the project.
Imagery: photography in this system is tenebrist — a single light source, a black ground, the subject in chiaroscuro or deep shadow. No flat product shots, no bright stock, no glamour. The subject is recovered from darkness, the way the mark is recovered from its ground.

RUBRIC · DESIGN SYSTEM Media · viii
Quies

Media & Motion

The discipline of stillness. A record does not animate. A page does not autoplay.

RUBRIC is print-first. The canonical artifact prints clean to paper — a page break after every section, exact colour adjustment forced, full-bleed grounds preserved. Motion is not a feature of the system; its absence is part of its weight.

Where an interface demands transition, the only permitted motion is the slow reveal: a single staggered fade on load, never looping, never bouncing, never calling attention to itself. No parallax, no hover theatrics, no spring physics. The model is a page turning, not an app responding.

Imagery follows the same logic — tenebrist, single-source, dark-ground, the subject in chiaroscuro. Sound, where it exists, is low and sparse: a struck bell, paper, near-silence. Never a notification tone, never a product chime.

Rule: if you are unsure whether an animation belongs, it does not. Stillness is the default and the discipline. The work is heavy; heavy things do not bounce.
RUBRIC · DESIGN SYSTEM Rejections · ix
Reiecta

What RUBRIC Refuses

The boundary is as much the system as the palette. These reads break it. Listed so they are never argued for again.
  • Gloss, bevel, and chrome Showroom polish is the visual signature of marketing. The only permitted shine is the forged mark's; finishes stay matte.
  • Rounded corners Soft radii read as software friendliness. Corners are cut. border-radius stays at zero.
  • A fourth typeface Three faces carry the whole system. A decorative display font or a second sans collapses the discipline.
  • Competing accents More than one saturated colour per page turns doctrine into packaging. Cross Red is rationed; nothing else competes.
  • Pure white and flat grounds White (#FFFFFF) and untextured fills break the parchment illusion. Grounds are warm and faintly stained.
  • Animation theatrics Parallax, bounce, spring, looping motion. The page turns; it does not perform.
  • Bright, generic stock imagery Evenly-lit photography on white defeats the tenebrist register. The subject is recovered from shadow or it is not used.
